<p><ul><li>DNase-Seq</li><li>FAIRE-SEQ</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Chromatin Immunoprecipitation Sequencing (ChIP-Seq) is a critical method for studying protein-DNA interactions in cells. Within this context, DNase-Seq and FAIRE-Seq are two prominent market types. DNase-Seq involves digesting chromatin with DNase I, identifying regions of open chromatin, while FAIRE-Seq (Formaldehyde-Assisted Isolation of Regulatory Elements) purifies DNA from cross-linked chromatin to identify accessible genomic regions. Both techniques provide insights into chromatin structure and gene regulation, catering to researchers focusing on epigenomics and transcriptional activities.</p></p>
