<p><p>The preeclampsia diagnostic market comprises two main types: blood tests and urine analysis. Blood tests primarily measure biomarkers, such as elevated levels of proteins and platelets, indicating potential preeclampsia. Conversely, urine analysis involves checking for protein levels, specifically proteinuria, which is a key indicator of the condition. Both methods are essential for timely diagnosis, facilitating early intervention to manage risks for both mother and baby.
