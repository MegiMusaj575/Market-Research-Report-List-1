<p><p>B-mode ultrasonography in the veterinary market encompasses various types that enhance diagnostic capabilities. Two-dimensional ultrasound provides real-time imaging for assessing organ structure and abnormalities in animals. Three-dimensional (3D) and four-dimensional (4D) ultrasonography offer volumetric imaging, allowing for detailed examinations of anatomical structures and dynamic processes. Doppler ultrasound is used to evaluate blood flow and vascular conditions, aiding in the assessment of cardiovascular health. Together, these technologies improve veterinary diagnostics and treatment planning.</p></p>
