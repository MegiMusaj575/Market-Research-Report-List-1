<p><p>The depression therapeutics market is primarily categorized into Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs), Selective Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs), and Other treatments. SSRIs, such as fluoxetine and sertraline, enhance serotonin levels to alleviate depressive symptoms. SNRIs, including venlafaxine and duloxetine, increase both serotonin and norepinephrine, targeting a broader spectrum of mood disorders. The "Others" category encompasses atypical antidepressants and alternative therapies, offering diverse options for patients with varying responses to traditional treatments. This segmentation allows for personalized approaches in managing depression.</p></p>
